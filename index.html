Final Conclusion

Round Robin guarantees that no process is starved — every process receives CPU time in a cyclic, time-bounded manner regardless of priority. This makes it ideal for interactive and time-sharing systems where fairness and consistent response time matter more than urgency.

Preemptive Priority Scheduling ensures that high-priority (urgent) processes run as soon as they arrive, even preempting a currently running lower-priority process. This is critical for real-time and mission-critical systems, but introduces a risk of starvation for low-priority processes if higher-priority ones arrive continuously.

The optimal choice depends on the workload: choose Round Robin for balanced fairness across all processes; choose Preemptive Priority when service differentiation, urgency, and task importance are the primary scheduling goals.
